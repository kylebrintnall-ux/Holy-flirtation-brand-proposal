i. The brief
In which we admit the problem

It is, frankly, rough out there.

Catholic singles, ages 22–35, want to meet each other on purpose — without the thumb‑flick, the parish‑coffee ambush, or the third‑party dating app that doesn't quite get it.

Holy Flirtation is a monthly mixer in Indianapolis. The brand needs to feel like a cocktail party hosted by your favorite reverent‑but‑hilarious aunt.

The job to be done

Make a serious thing feel inviting. Make a sacred thing feel fun.

So the wallflower, the convert, the cradle‑Catholic, and the recovering app‑user all show up — and stay through the end of the speed round.

What it is not

Not a parish bulletin. Not a dating app. Not pious wallpaper. Not a Hallmark store.

ii. Brand promise
Three pillars · one Holy Spirit
The promise, in seven words

Reverent. Romantic. Ridiculous.

i.

Faithful, not fussy.

The Catholic stuff is a feature, not a footnote. We talk about saints, encyclicals, and the rosary on the wing — without making the room into a catechism class.

ii.

Warm, with edge.

Cream paper, candlelight palette, hand‑lettered wordmark — set against a punchy sunset gradient that signals: this is a party, and you're invited.

iii.

Funny, on purpose.

Every line of copy earns its place. We write like the friend who does the homily‑voice impression on the drive home — fond, sharp, and impossible to misread as sincere bait.

vii. The patron
Watercolor & pencil · loose, never literal · always one beat from a punchline

Meet our patron. He is, technically, the Holy Spirit — and He has many faces.

— "the wink" · primary portrait, hero use only
— the rosary
— the lurker
— the messenger
— in flight

He is rendered loose: pencil and watercolor on warm paper, with a permanent half‑smile and a faint sense that He has heard your last text and is choosing to be polite about it.

Used at scale, He's the punctuation — never the headline. One bird per layout, please.

— four pillars · one Tuesday a week
i.Mondays
Testimonials, but not the cringey kind.

Couples who met at a chapter event tell the story plainly — what they wore, what they ate, who introduced them. No engagement‑announcement Reels.

ii.Wednesdays
From the priests.

Short editorials by parish priests — on courtship, chastity, intentionality, and the difference between a meet‑cute and a sacrament. One per month, max.

iii.Fridays
From the married couples.

Tips from people 4–40 years in: how to ask, how to break up like a Christian, how to tell your mother. Funny, specific, never advice‑columny.

iv.Sundays
Theology of the body, in plain English.

A serial reading of John Paul II's Wednesday audiences — one per week, written for the room, not the seminary. The thought‑leadership engine.
